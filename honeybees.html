HoneyBees

Honeybees are highly social, flying insects belonging to the genus Apis, renowned for their production and storage of honey and their vital role in pollination. They live in complex, cooperative colonies, or hives, which are centered around a rigid social structure with a clear division of labor. The colony's smooth operation and survival are dependent on a remarkable communication system involving both chemical signals, known as pheromones, and a unique "waggle dance".
